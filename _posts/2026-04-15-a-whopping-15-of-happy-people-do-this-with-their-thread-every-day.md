In a groundbreaking study that has left scientists in stitches, it turns out that a whopping 15% of happy people engage in a hilarious yet heartwarming activity with their thread every single day. They are, dear reader, knitting miniature sweaters for their pet rocks!

In a world where we're constantly chasing the newest tech gadget, these joyous folks have found happiness in something as simple as a bit of yarn and a smidge of creativity. "It's the little things in life, like dressing up pebbles in adorable knitwear," said one participant, grinning from ear to ear as they put a mini cardigan on a speckled granite stone.

The thread enthusiasts were discovered by a group of researchers from the [International Institute of Joy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Happiness), who had set out to uncover the secrets to a happy life. What they didn't expect to find was a growing community of people who have not only chosen to adopt rocks as pets, but also to knit them a daily wardrobe change.

"It's a real rock and roll lifestyle," one researcher joked, shaking their head in disbelief. "We never expected to find such joy in the sedimentary simplicity of Stone Age fashion!"

The research also found that these happy knitters have a special name for their community: "The Rocking Stitchers". They meet online every week to swap knitting patterns, exchange photos of their fashion-forward rocks and share laughter.

"We 'rock' the knitting world!" said a Rocking Stitcher. "It's about finding joy in the silliest of things. Plus, rocks don't complain about patterns or colors. They're the perfect fashion models!"

So the next time you're feeling a little down, why not grab a thread and your favourite pebble? You may discover that happiness is just a few stitches away. After all, a pet rock in a hand-knitted sweater is a stone-cold recipe for a good laugh and a happy heart.

And who knows? You might just end up joining the 15% of happiest people in the world. As the Rocking Stitchers like to say, "Don't take life for granite, stitch some joy into it!"
